Chinese Innovation Project

Anticipating Sputnik Moments – Chinese Innovation and Critical Technologies

Description:

This project investigates the technological competition between the United States and China, with a focus on emerging critical and dual-use technologies such as hypersonics. The goal is to provide insight into China’s rapid innovation advancements and their implications for U.S. national security policy. By analyzing comprehensive patent data, the project identifies disruptive technologies, evaluates the role of Chinese government interventions, measures U.S.-China technological decoupling, and recommends strategic policy responses.

Data Description and Construction

To support the research, the team constructed a new, comprehensive database of Chinese invention patents—a feat necessitated by the lack of a unified, publicly accessible Chinese patent database.

Data Sources:
  • European Patent Office’s PATSTAT Global dataset
  • Google Patents
  • China National Intellectual Property Administration (CNIPA) database (accessed from within China)
Coverage:
  • Patents granted from September 1985 through September 2023
  • Focused on invention patents only (excluding design and utility models)
Cleaning and Standardization Steps:
  • Deduplication of multiple publications for the same patent
  • Normalization of forward citations to assess patent impact
  • Disambiguation of assignee identities
  • Correction for citation inconsistencies (e.g., citing applications instead of grants)
Patent Importance Measures:
  • Forward citations normalized by technology and grant date
  • Textual novelty, using NLP techniques to assess innovation uniqueness
Technology Mapping:

Mapped patents to critical technologies using the U.S. Defense Technical Information Center (DTIC) classification system (251 subfields, keyword-based).

Scope:
  • Final dataset includes 14.1 million Chinese domestic invention patent publications
  • Allows for disaggregation by entity type (e.g., military, universities, SOEs, VC-backed firms)

This dataset underpins four main research aims: identifying emerging technologies, assessing Chinese policy influence, evaluating technological decoupling, and recommending U.S. policy responses.
